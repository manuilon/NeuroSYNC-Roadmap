Workshop Readout

Industrial Applications: setting the commercial bar

Conference Aston — 28 April 2026 · What we heard from 64 participant worksheets · Readout issued May 2026

The Industrial Applications workshop was held at Conference Aston on 28 April 2026, ahead of the Research Workshop. Its purpose was to move the roadmap's industrial applications chapter from "what is possible" to "what is required" — defining the technical, economic and manufacturing thresholds neuromorphic systems must cross for enterprise-scale adoption, and testing candidate strategic themes rather than presenting a finished list of use cases. It was run as a red-team exercise across three blocks, each combining individual worksheet capture with group resolution debate; the findings below are drawn from the 64 worksheets collected.

The contradiction we asked industry to help resolve

Stakeholder feedback going in pointed to strong appetite for advanced Edge AI, bottlenecked by legacy-system integration and device variability. We asked participants to help set hard technical red lines: power limits, latency bounds, throughput constraints and legacy-integration bridges required to displace conventional systems — plus the TRL and benchmarking milestones needed to unlock private capital and cross the commercial "valley of death."
